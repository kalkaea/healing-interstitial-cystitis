Gentle Movement

Movement supports lymphatic drainage, circulation, and nervous system regulation, all of which contribute to healing. The emphasis here is on gentle.

High-impact exercise or anything stressful on the pelvic floor can trigger flares in the early stages, so walking, gentle yoga, swimming, and light cycling are ideal. Twenty to thirty minutes of movement most days is enough.

10,000 steps, heavy lifting, and hot yoga are excellent for a healthy body. A body in crisis is not a healthy body right now — it is a body under significant physiological load. High-impact exercise elevates cortisol, stresses the pelvic floor, and pushes a nervous system that is already in overdrive further into defense mode. There will be a time for all of it. This is not that time.

Restorative Sleep

Sleep is when the body does the majority of its repair work, including the repair of the bladder lining.

Chronic sleep deprivation elevates cortisol, keeps the nervous system in a reactive state, and undermines every other healing effort being made. Seven to nine hours of quality sleep is a necessary part of your healing protocol.

A consistent sleep schedule, a dark room, no screens in the hour before bed, and a wind-down practice are not optional lifestyle additions. Reading, a bath, or a cup of herbal tea in the hour before bed are all simple ways to begin signalling to your body that it is safe to rest.

If you are working through the IC Healing Academy Safe and Sound program for nervous system repair, the wind-down hour is an excellent time to incorporate that practice.
