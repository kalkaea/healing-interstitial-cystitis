Soothing the Next 48 Hours

Nourishment

During a flare, remove any foods you know are personal triggers.

For most people this includes acidic foods like tomatoes, citrus, coffee, and spicy food.

Think bland, warm, and gentle. Jasmine rice, oats, sourdough bread, well-sourced meats, healthy fats, and gentle fruits like apples, pears, peaches, and mango are all good choices during this window. Your digestive system is under stress alongside your bladder, and giving it less to work with helps both.

I don’t believe in the IC Diet or intense elimination diets, these have shown to do more harm than good in the long run, as it does not address why the bladder is reactive in the first place.

If you go on to work with the healing plan, I will walk you through an approach to nutrition that gradually reduces oxalate load and accounts for sulfur, in a way that actively supports healing rather than just managing flares.

Mindset

Pick one thing you are already doing today and do it on purpose.

Make the tea slowly. Fold the laundry consciously, with intention. Take the bath without planning tomorrow.

Most people move through recovery on autopilot: mentally somewhere else, running the worry loop, tracking symptoms, rehearsing worst-case scenarios. That mental state is a choice, even when it doesn’t feel like one, and it has a direct impact on how you experience the next 48 hours.

Intentionality is not a personality trait. It is a practice, and this is a simple place to start.
