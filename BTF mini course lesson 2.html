Lesson Two

More than ten years ago, IC took over my life. This is how I got it back.

Michelle Burbidge, C-IHP

Certified Integrative Healing Practitioner

More than two years of my life disappeared into this illness. Not gradually, it happened fast, and it took everything with it. After what I thought was “that one UTI” I went from running a successful business and being fully present for my young daughter, to spending entire days on the couch, unable to eat more than a handful of foods without paying for it. I was managing panic attacks that had become a daily reality, and living with pain that was relentless and exhausting in a way that is genuinely difficult to describe to someone who has not been there.

The foods I had eaten my whole life became something I was afraid of. Plans I had made became pipe dreams, and my passion for dancing, laughter, and homeschooling dwindled to barely embers.

The life I had built started shrinking around me, and the medical system I turned to for help had very little to offer beyond a diagnosis, a prescription, and the strong implication that I should start adjusting my expectations.

I did not accept that. But I also had no idea, at that point, what the alternative was going to look like or how long it was going to take to find it.

My Search

I exhausted every option available to me.

I saw five different urologists. I travelled to see the one who was supposed to be the best in the field. He was no better than the others. I tried pelvic floor therapy. I did everything I was told to do and followed every recommendation that was made. I exhausted every option the mainstream medical system had to offer, and then watched it drop me anyway.

My doctor looked at me at one point and said: “I think you have to understand that this is just your life now.” And shrugged. I had put my complete trust in a system that had run out of things to try and seemed entirely unbothered by that fact.

So I turned to naturopaths. The first told me I needed to cut out dairy and gluten. The second told me I needed to cut out meat and try to go raw. A third, after some IgG testing, looked at the five foods I was still tolerating and removed two more of them. I was exhausted and at my wit’s end. Each time I went back I was less healthy than the time before, and not one of them could tell me why or what to do about it.

I tried practitioners who used methods I could not verify or understand. I spent money I did not have on approaches that could not explain themselves.

And then I found someone on YouTube.

I watched every video she posted. I read every comment she made. I followed her work for almost a year before I finally reached out, because by that point I had already spent so much, financially and emotionally, that I could not afford to be wrong again. It was not really the money. It was the hope. I could not put my hopes into one more practitioner who did not know what they said they knew. Finding out they did not was too painful to risk again.

What made her different was that she could explain everything. She ran actual lab tests and knew precisely what each result meant and what to do about it. Every question I asked was answered clearly and directly. I only needed to hear from one person that getting out of this was possible. That was all I needed to go all in.

What Happened Next

The first thing she did was test my hormones.

What she found immediately reframed everything. I was highly estrogen dominant, because I was not detoxing it properly. My symptoms had looked like low estrogen, so my previous naturopath had prescribed me more estrogen without any testing, which had pushed that value even higher and made everything worse.

She found that my cortisol was not actually low, as I had been told by another practitioner who had based that conclusion entirely on my symptoms. She found extreme nutrient deficiencies that had developed, in large part, because of the diet I had been following so carefully. My mostly-vegetable, plant-heavy approach that I genuinely believed was keeping me healthy was driving a significant portion of my symptoms.

She helped me correct my sleep, rebalance my brain chemistry, and address the specific deficiencies that were keeping my body from being able to repair itself. But the shift that changed everything first was food fear.

She helped me get past my fear of dairy, meat, and carbohydrates, and helped me understand that the IC Diet I had been following so carefully was doing me more harm than good. Once I had that piece sorted, in conjunction with the mindset work we were doing at the same time, my symptoms began to come down within about three months.

It was not a straight line. There were good weeks and bad ones, and the progress felt uneven while it was happening. But the overall pain trajectory kept moving downward over the following year, until one day I simply realized I was not in any pain anymore.

The thing she helped me understand above all else was that symptom suppression was never going to be the goal. Suppressing a symptom and resolving what is producing it are two entirely different things, and every approach I had tried before her had been working at the wrong level.

It took a little over a year. I was fortunate in that I had not had IC for very long at that point, and I had not yet undergone the heavier invasive procedures that make recovery harder and longer. For some women it does take more time, and I want to be honest about that.

Where I Went From There

In the end, my business did survive and thrive again.

But by the time I was well, I already knew I was going in a different direction. What had happened to me, the years of being dismissed, the dead ends, the women I had watched limping from one flare to the next with no real way forward, was happening to thousands of others. I wanted to become the practitioner that I had so desperately needed when I was where they are now.

I had studied neuro-, psycho-, and cognitive linguistics at university and had continued that work independently for years, which gave me a foundation in how language and thought patterns shape the body’s responses. Eventually I spent three years training with my original practitioner to learn the clinical side of what she does.

I have now walked dozens of women through this process, women who were told IC was incurable, who had been on elimination diets for years, who had tried every supplement, who had undergone instillations and procedures that gave them no lasting relief, and many of them are already pain-free. Not managing symptoms, but genuinely healed.
