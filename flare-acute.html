Flare Management

Acute Flare Tools

Our goal is not to manage symptoms forever. But no one likes to be in pain, and these tools will help you get more comfortable without hindering your healing progress.

These tools are for when a flare is actively happening. They are not a healing plan, but a short-term relief designed to get you more comfortable while the deeper work continues. Use them as needed.

Best completed in this order

1

Slow Your Breathing

Your nervous system is in alarm right now and that is making everything feel worse than it needs to. The fastest way to change that is to change your breathing. A slow exhale, longer than your inhale, signals the nervous system to stand down. Try four counts in and six counts out. Do this for two to three minutes before you do anything else. It costs nothing and it works.

2

Baking Soda Water

This temporarily alkalizes the urine and reduces the burning sensation during a flare. It is not a treatment. It is a bridge to get you more comfortable while your body settles. Mix a quarter teaspoon into a full glass of filtered water and drink it slowly. You can repeat this up to three times across the day. Do not use it long term.

Note: If you have high blood pressure, check with your doctor before using this. If sodium is a concern, potassium bicarbonate is an alternative but check with your practitioner before switching.
3

Marshmallow Root or Slippery Elm Tea

This cold infusion needs to be made the night before, so if you do not have it ready, make it tonight for tomorrow. The mucilage in these herbs coats the bladder lining and reduces irritation from the inside. It is one of the most effective soothing tools available, but often overlooked because it is not a pill.

To make it: add one heaping tablespoon of marshmallow root or slippery elm to 16 ounces of cool filtered water. Cover it and leave it overnight for 8 to 12 hours. Strain it in the morning and sip it slowly throughout the day. You will know it is ready when the liquid feels slightly thick. That texture is the active part.
